\subsection{Detailed Description}
\subsubsection*{template$<$typename R\+E\+A\+L\+\_\+\+T, typename E\+V\+A\+L\+\_\+\+T = R\+E\+A\+L\+\_\+\+T$>$class mas\+::\+Subpopulation$<$ R\+E\+A\+L\+\_\+\+T, E\+V\+A\+L\+\_\+\+T $>$}

~\newline
 The subpopulation class follows the well-\/established premise of conservation of biomass ~\newline
 (Russell 1931) within each area of the model. The general model of the time change, indexed by ~\newline
 t , in the biomass ( a,t B ) of the harvested subpopulation in each area, indexed by a , depends on ~\newline
 biomass increases due to somatic growth (G(a,t)), recruitment (R(a,t)), and immigration (I(a,t)) and ~\newline
 depends on biomass decreases due to natural deaths (D(a,t)), fishery yields (Y(a,t)), and emigration ~\newline
 (O(a,t) ) via ~\newline
 ~\newline
 B(a,t+1) = B(a,t) + \mbox{[}G(a,t) + R(a,t) + I(a,t)\mbox{]} -\/ \mbox{[}D(a,t) + Y(a,t) + O(a,t)\mbox{]}
